## Design Context

### Users

Panda is for technically fluent users working inside a single project context in
the browser. The primary audience spans solo builders shipping quickly, small
teams reviewing and coordinating work, and power users managing multiple runs or
tasks inside one project. They arrive focused on getting from prompt to
inspected result with minimal friction and strong operational clarity.

### Brand Personality

Focused, operational, premium technical. Panda should feel calm under load,
trustworthy during risky actions, and precise rather than flashy. It should read
as a serious instrument for software work, not an AI toy or a generic browser
IDE.

### Aesthetic Direction

Retain Panda's brutalist DNA: sharp corners, strong structure, monospace
discipline, and explicit state. Refine it into a quieter, more premium shell
with better hierarchy, calmer tonal separation, and less dashboard repetition.
Anti-goals: do not look like a generic AI IDE, do not drift into enterprise PM
software, and do not become visually noisy.

### Design Principles

1. Prioritize work state over tool chrome.
2. Keep the interface calm, explicit, and reviewable during execution.
3. Preserve brutalist structure while refining spacing, hierarchy, and surface
   tone.
4. Use color and emphasis for operational meaning, not decoration.
5. Make the next action obvious without overwhelming power users.
